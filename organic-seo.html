Organic SEO and Why It Is Important?

Organic SEO is the practice of using white hat SEO strategies to boost your site traffic and improve your website’s presence in non-paid search engine results.

The word organic is used to differentiate between natural search engine optimization methods and paid strategies for rankings in SERPs.

Understanding Google’s SERP Features

When you have a question you want to answer like “ what is the best dishwasher” or “french fries near me”, where do you turn?

Google, Yahoo, or some other search engine.

You are not only the one, with the majority of website clicks, but around 68% also create with a search on a search engine. When you say or type something into a search engine results page (SERP) is what you receive.

A typical SERP will provide search users with two different types of content, pay-per-click Ads knowns as “paid search” and organic search results.

Appearing in the organic search results is particularly valuable in comparison to the paid results, organic search results receive relatively more clicks. Google also comes with a host of SERP features like rich snippets, maps, knowledge panels, and video results. All of these results kinds excluding sponsored Ads are affected by organic SEO techniques. When you compare today’s SERPs to the original 1998 Google search result page, today’s SERPs are very complex.

How Does Organic SEO Work?

The results that search engines give to their users are not chosen randomly or occur by chance. Each search engine uses a proprietary, complicated algorithm to determine what results would perfectly answer a search user’s question. Without too much detail, search engine employ crawlers to scour the internet and collect information. A crawler will gather all kinds of content from visual to text.

To find this content a crawler starts from a known web page through external and internal links. Crawlers will try to comprehend the context of each linked web page.

The information gathered about a web page is included in the search engine’s database, also called a search index. Each web page is cataloged in its context.

Difference between Paid Search And Organic SEO

The current SERPs are really competitive than they were in the past,

Nearly 93% of Google users click on results they find on the first page. The second page of Google just sees 0.78% clicks. That is a large difference in click-through rates (CTRs) for businesses.

This large difference in CTRs is the main reason Organic SEO is more important than ever.

Since every business competes for user attention in the SERPs, every company is trying hard to get their business to show in the first ten results of any organic SERP.

But some companies who fall behind in their SEO endeavors with the competition want faster entry to that oh-so-precious first-page result. To “Earn” those posts, businesses will bid on particular keywords and pay for paid Advertisements.

The results you see above the regular organic search listings are called pay-per-click Ads (PPC).
